## Why it exists

Money is the job.

The first war room read made the business picture clearer: Sick is strongest when it organizes around event launches, subscription growth, impulse add-ons, hero merch SKUs, and cleaner offer structure.

The operating layer exists to make that insight usable every day instead of leaving it as a smart one-time analysis.

This page is the bridge between:

- the strategic revenue picture
- the current merch and inventory reality
- the next concrete move the team should make today

## Revenue engine

Current read from the earlier analysis:

- 90-day total sales around $903.4k
- biggest drivers were Online Store, POS, and subscriptions
- translation: the business is powered by online sales, event/POS energy, and recurring subscription revenue

## What is actually winning

The strategic pass says the business does best when it leans into:

- ticket and event energy
- magazine subscriptions and recurring revenue
- stickers, hats, and other easy add-on items
- a short list of hero merch SKUs instead of the whole catalog fighting for attention
- clean offer structure instead of fuzzy promotion

## Current hot zones

The earlier read flagged these as the current attention zones:

- Speedfest
- Sick Summer 2026
- Syclone giveaway
- Magazine subscriptions

These should shape campaign weight before random catalog pushes do.

## Current merch attack model

For day-to-day merch execution:

- pick one hero product, not ten equal products
- pair it with one sticker or add-on bundle to lift AOV
- drive one clear angle across site, email, and social the same day
- update a tiny scorecard daily so momentum is visible
- rotate creative based on hooks, not random guessing

## Board lanes

Use the war room in four operating lanes:

### Lane 1: Revenue now

- ticket launches
- Speedfest
- Sick Summer 2026
- any active premium offer that deserves campaign-level attention

### Lane 2: Retention / recurring

- quarterly subscriptions
- annual subscriptions
- upsells that turn one-time buyers into repeat revenue

### Lane 3: Merch winners

- hats
- stickers
- keychains
- Sick Spit
- giveaway merch
- any current hero SKU with enough stock and enough proof to push

### Lane 4: Cleanup

- reporting fixes
- discount / code attribution
- SKU rationalization
- separating evergreen merch from event merch and promo offers

## Main war room read

The business is not strongest when every product gets equal emotional importance.

It is strongest when the board organizes around:

1. event launches
2. subscription growth
3. impulse add-on merch
4. hero merch SKUs
5. clean offer structure

## Risks and weak spots

- discount attribution is still weak from the current export
- catalog noise is likely too high
- long-tail merch can steal attention from better offers
- evergreen merch, event merch, and promo / lead-gen offers need cleaner separation
- low-stock and out-of-stock winners can distort what looks promotable if the board is not kept current

## Non-negotiables

- one main push at a time
- every campaign needs a hook
- every hook needs a KPI
- do not let the whole catalog compete equally
- if a product is not moving, change the angle, image, placement, or offer quickly
- log experiments so the same bad idea does not get repeated as a new idea

## Morning questions

- What are we selling today?
- Why should someone care today?
- Is this a revenue-now push, a retention push, a merch-winner push, or a cleanup move?
- Where will they see it first?
- What add-on increases cart value?
- What number tells us by tonight if it worked?
